About UIRIMAL & Morocco

UIRIMAL: UIR Overview

As the first public-private partnership in higher education, the Université Internationale de Rabat (UIR) embodies a unique model in Morocco by being non-profit, State-funded and managed by a private entity. This structure enables the university to excel in academic training and research, contributing to the socio-economic development of the Kingdom and the region. In identifying and developing the twelve schools and departments that make up UIR, the university looked toward the Moroccan diaspora abroad to join the institution as deans, faculty and administration, contributing to a diverse and international academic environment. From its first incoming classes, UIR’s vision sought to encourage socio-economic diversity, thus creating one of the first scholarship and financial aid structures in Morocco and encouraging students from all backgrounds to enroll in fully accredited Bachelors and Masters programs.

 

With nearly 2,000 students enrolled, the university has facilitated several academic and research partnerships outside of Morocco, with Sciences Po Grenoble, ESIC Madrid, Georgia Institute of Technology and Mississippi State University in offering double-degree programs, all with the objective of enriching the educational experience and encouraging cross-disciplinary mobility and collaboration for students and faculty in fields ranging from Political Science and Architecture to Aerospace Engineering and Renewable Energy.  Outside of the campus, the university has formed strong ties with the local community in Rabat and Morocco, including with research institutes such as the Centre Jacques Berque and the National Library. In 2014, UIR was awarded the “International Quality Crown” for its advancement in innovation and academic quality and excellence, followed by the “Prix de l’Innovation” by the Moroccan Authority on Industrial and Commercial Property and the Ministry of Education, for its strategic research and development emphasis, resulting in over 100 patents in two years. The campus construction planning integrated this priority of being “green”, resulting in an HQE-certified administrative building, a solar farm and a water basin irrigation system.  These awards and efforts reflect the university’s investment in advancing scientific research, scholarly publication and intellectual property.

 

UIR, with its students and faculty is committed to mobilizing stakeholders and resources in organizing UIRIMAL sessions under the theme of New Paths, New Challenges. Hosting an event of this magnitude, with a network of students, educators and knowledge-seekers significantly contributes to UIR’s institutional vision, in highlighting the importance of Morocco as a destination for international scholarship and reinforcing the importance of a global mindset for the student body, faculty and campus environment.

 

 

Morocco & Rabat

 

Morocco offers an exciting mix of cultures, sitting at the crossroads of Arabic, European and Amazigh cultures. Most Moroccans are trilingual and the country’s location at the crossroads between East and West, North and South, has fostered a long history resulting in wide-ranging trade, student mobility and international exchange with its Mediterranean neighbors, the Middle East and beyond. In 2014 Morocco hosted President Obama’s initiative, the Global Entrepreneurship Summit in Marrakech, bringing together hundreds of nationalities in an event focused on developing leadership, entrepreneurship and cultivating international collaborations. This was followed by the Global Forum on Human Rights, again attracting thousands of participants to convene and address global advances and challenges in the field.  Rabat is equally a fascinating cosmopolitan city where the old and new local and world cultures blend harmoniously, with the city featuring a beautiful mixture of modern lifestyles and cultural traditions. Having just been named by the New York Times one of the top destinations for 2015, Rabat hopes to welcome visitors to share its wealth of history and modernity.

 

 

 

 

UIR Campus Overview, Facilities & Capacity

 

Campus Map

 

Overview

 

The Université Internationale de Rabat is built on approximately fifty acres of land and neighbors Rabat-Salé’s Technopolis, with a campus that is continuously expanding to eventually take up thirty of those acres. Currently the campus hosts three academic and administrative buildings, each with classrooms, auditoriums, research laboratories, office space and meeting rooms.  These buildings were designed with an open atrium, which has been transformed for special events like the UIR graduation ceremony and the visit of France’s President Hollande. Alongside these, UIR will be opening an additional dedicated center for scientific research and laboratory work. UIR’s Engineering and Renewable Energy students intend to work with partner Université de Lorraine in France to open a FabLab workshop for digital fabrication, based on a model originating at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

 

In addition, the campus has four dorms, each with a capacity of housing 415 students, a library, cafeteria and a gym with a semi-Olympic pool, several football fields and basketball and tennis courts. The library equally houses a space for UIR’s Executive and Continuing Education programs and is planned to also integrate the UIR Career Center and a language lab. There is also access to a small grocery store, a café, student lounges, a doctor’s office and a laundromat on campus.

 

All buildings on campus are equipped with WIFI and academic classrooms are equipped with computers and projectors. UIR has technical staff prepared to provide technical setup and support for conference organization as well as a general operations team that assists with setup of displays, materials, food arrangements and other needs.

Accessibility: Airport Arrival & Travel Information

 

Conference participants would have the option of flying into Casablanca International Airport (CMN), which offers some direct flights with the United States through Royal Air Maroc.  Casablanca’s airport is approximately 80 miles or a one and a half hour drive from UIR. The UIR conference organizing committee will provide options for transportation to the campus.

 

Additionally, participants can opt to fly into the smaller Rabat-Salé Airport (RBA), which is about eight miles or a fifteen minute drive from UIR. Flights to RBA from the U.S. can be arranged through Delta/Air France, with a layover in Paris or through Iberia and Royal Air Maroc, with a stop in Madrid or Barcelona.

 

Flight time to Morocco depends on the city of origin, but is comparable in price and duration as destinations in Europe, notably France, Spain and England, with a flight from New York to Casablanca at about seven hours and around $800 to $1,000 non-stop. Participants coming from the United States and most European countries do not require tourist visas to enter Morocco. More information about tourist visas can be found at http://www.moroccanconsulate.com/visa.cfm.

 

The UIR staff will prepare a list of options for participants interested in renting cars and any other services upon arrival. 

 

For attendees interested in extending their trip to visit other parts of Morocco, we would be pleased to provide information and options for Fes, Meknes, Marrakech and Essaouira, as well as a trip to the Sahara Desert.
